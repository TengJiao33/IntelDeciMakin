# APSS innovation wording v3

Date: 2026-06-29

Reason for revision: the earlier wording still sounded like claims or positions. Based on peer contribution samples, each contribution should be phrased as a concrete object: method, representation, variable, index, algorithm, or decision-support output.

## What to avoid

Avoid contribution wording like:

- We formulate preference-structure uncertainty as an important issue.
- We propose an adaptive MCDM framework.
- We consider both predictive performance and interpretability.
- We provide a benchmark for adaptive decision making.

These are claims, motivations, or validation supports. They are not yet contribution objects.

## Recommended contribution structure

### Contribution 1: Preference-Structure Representation with learnable structure variables

Peer-like wording:

> We introduce a Preference-Structure Representation that encodes criteria relevance, criterion monotonicity type, and pairwise interaction activation as learnable structure variables in preference disaggregation.

What the object is:

- criterion relevance variable: whether criterion `g_j` is used;
- criterion-shape variable: whether `g_j` is gain, cost, single-peaked, single-valley, or flexible nonmonotone;
- interaction activation variable: whether pair `(g_i, g_j)` contributes an interaction term;
- optional aggregation-family indicator: additive, sparse-additive, nonmonotone, or interaction-aware structure.

Why this is more like peer writing:

- Zhou et al. introduce 0-1 variables to indicate criteria selection.
- Minoungou et al. introduce variables for criterion type in MR-Sort.
- Herin et al. learn sparse interaction terms.
- Our object combines these structural variables into one representation for preference-structure learning.

What not to claim:

- Do not say we are first to learn criteria, nonmonotonicity, or interactions separately.
- Say instead that we provide a unified representation for these structure components within adaptive preference disaggregation.

### Contribution 2: Structure-Gated Preference Disaggregation algorithm

Peer-like wording:

> We develop a Structure-Gated Preference Disaggregation algorithm that jointly estimates preference-model parameters and activates higher-order structure components only when they improve fit under stability and parsimony constraints.

What the object is:

- an algorithm, not a philosophical framework;
- it starts from a simple additive baseline;
- it evaluates candidate structural expansions;
- it activates criteria, nonmonotone forms, or interaction terms through gates;
- it outputs a compact supported preference structure plus learned parameters.

Possible algorithmic components:

- additive baseline learner;
- criteria gate;
- monotonicity / shape gate;
- interaction gate;
- stability filter across splits or perturbations;
- parsimony penalty;
- final supported structure set.

Why this is more like peer writing:

- Sobrie et al. propose a population-based metaheuristic.
- Zhou et al. develop an embedded criteria selection model.
- Martyn & Kadziński develop MCDA-inspired neural architectures.
- Our contribution should similarly be a named procedure that can be implemented and ablated.

What not to claim:

- Do not say simply "we make MCDM adaptive."
- Say the algorithm learns a compact structure configuration from assignment examples.

### Contribution 3: Preference-Structure Acceptability Index and structure-robust recommendation outputs

Peer-like wording:

> We introduce Preference-Structure Acceptability Index and structure-necessary / structure-possible recommendation outputs to quantify whether a decision recommendation is stable across supported preference structures.

What the object is:

- Preference-Structure Acceptability Index: empirical support of each candidate structure or structure family;
- structure-necessary recommendation: a ranking, assignment, or choice shared by all sufficiently supported structures;
- structure-possible recommendation: a ranking, assignment, or choice produced by at least one sufficiently supported structure;
- central preference structure: a representative structure explanation, not merely a representative parameter vector.

Why this is more like peer writing:

- Pelissari & Duarte introduce Scenario Acceptability Index and Scenario Central Capacity vector.
- ROR/SMAA output necessary/possible or acceptability information within a fixed model family.
- Our object moves this style of robustness output from parameter instances to preference structures.

What not to claim:

- Do not present this as "validation".
- Present it as a decision-support output of the method.

## Proposed final contribution paragraph

The paper's contribution paragraph could be written as:

> This study makes three contributions. First, we introduce a Preference-Structure Representation that encodes criteria relevance, criterion monotonicity type, and pairwise interaction activation as learnable structure variables in preference disaggregation. Second, we develop a Structure-Gated Preference Disaggregation algorithm that jointly estimates preference-model parameters and activates higher-order structure components only when they improve fit under stability and parsimony constraints. Third, we introduce a Preference-Structure Acceptability Index together with structure-necessary and structure-possible recommendation outputs, enabling analysts to assess whether a sorting, ranking, or choice recommendation is robust across supported preference structures rather than only across parameter instances of a preselected model.

## One-sentence story

The concise story is:

> Instead of asking analysts to preselect an MCDA preference structure and then learn only its parameters, this paper represents, learns, and reports the decision consequences of preference structures themselves.

## Current ranking of these three

1. Contribution 2 is the main method contribution.
2. Contribution 3 is the decision-journal signature contribution.
3. Contribution 1 is the enabling representation contribution.

This order may be better for the paper:

1. Structure-Gated Preference Disaggregation algorithm.
2. Preference-Structure Representation with learnable structure variables.
3. Preference-Structure Acceptability Index and structure-robust recommendation outputs.

But in the introduction, it may read more naturally as representation -> algorithm -> output.

## Remaining risk

The main collision risks are:

- criteria selection: Zhou et al. 2025;
- criterion type learning: Minoungou et al. 2021;
- sparse interaction learning: Herin et al. 2023;
- taxonomy-based method selection: Cinelli et al. 2022;
- ANN-based multi-MCDA preference learning: Martyn & Kadziński 2023.

Therefore, the claim should be carefully scoped:

> The novelty is not any single structure learner alone, but the combination of a unified structure-variable representation, a gated preference-disaggregation algorithm, and structure-level acceptability outputs for decision recommendations.
